there are many things i want to say but they are all jumbled up in my head. so lets make a list of things i've been thinking about:

  • i've had a hard time applying boundaries i've set to friendships. i really want to cultivate better friendships in the future and to spend more time with people who actually care about me. this blog post by arachne has been a really good read on the topic of friendships!
  • been enjoying my new webdev job. but i also find it pretty hard at times. and it's not even that the task itself is hard. sometimes i know what i want to do but because i am so new to backend and the tools we use, i end up taking up a lot of time when all i had to do was write like 3 lines of code MAX. it's little things like that that are a bit frustrating. but i guess it is part of the journey, and on the bright side: i am learning lots of new things!
  • i am quite a bit scared for this upcoming uni semester with calculus 2 and operating systems on my plate... alongside my job which takes me 20 hrs per week. i know i will manage. SOMEHOW. but i have a feeling that this semester will be Rough for me.
  • been noticing lately how hard it is for me to spend time on video games as of late. but more specificically: on time consuming games due to difficulty. it's very frustrating because i WANT to play these games. i think it is because i get anxious about wasting my time on something without making any progress while having a couple of other more productive things i could be doing instead. it sounds silly but i've found that a decent way of combating this is just to put a 1hr or 30mins timer while playing said game. i recently did this with silksong because i got a bit stuck on the last judge boss fight. i made myself play the game for 30mins each day to just go and have a try at the boss. and i think doing made be even more aware of the little progress i was doing each day. by the 3rd day i managed to beat her.

huge fan of observing and noticing things. now i gotta work on them!
